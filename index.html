Exploratory data analysis with anomaly detection example

August 02, 2020

Last modified Feb 19, 2021

Similarly as missings in data, anomalies occurs very often especially while processing big data, more precisely known as 5V or 8V. These kind of observations leads to pose scores of complications in further modeling and could mislead final statistical inferences. It other words, it's impossible to create well performed statistical model or any good research without this essential step in process of analyse. Many solutions exists, it can be erased or changed but this may tamper the data. Depending of purposes of use the data choosing and applying adequate methods can be helpful, however care and precision are highly required. Some of them are based on overall assumptions, others are more specific, more depends on structure or content of the dataset and goal of diagnosis.

I created an example of exploratory data analysis with one more common method and one less common of detecting outliers. This was performed on data containing Brasilian houses to rent. The map below points on cities in with the analysed offers are located.

Important to mention is, analysis was perfomed on Databrick's platform, which leveraged Spark's distributed processing power while handlling computations. All has been done in Python and a bit of Scala language. In this case, two methods i used for detecting outliers was an interquartile range and the isolation forest from sci-kit learn library. The first one is pure statistics method while IsolationForest is a tree-based model where partitions are created by first randomly selecting a feature and then selecting random value in range between minimum and maximum of that feature. Description of data exploration was performed by major steps with a little conclusion.

Tap on the upper green bar "View projects" and from appeared list choose Anomaly detection & EDA example section. You should get instantly directed to the analysis.

MeteoApp Dashboard

July 19, 2020

Last update Mar 01, 2021

MeteoApp it's a data dashboard that allows to track current weather information in Poland. The first and main idea was to simplified presentation of the data to show only vital indicators that everyone's using on the daily basis. Another crucial step was extend the basic data by develop statistical model, which can create forecasts on the fly. This is perform in relation to historical air temperature data in Warsaw. Forecast can be run by switching on the forecast button in the MeteApp, located under datatable. With latest update the accuracy of model prediction has been increased, thus forecast performs much better now.

To open MeteoApp, tap on the upper green bar "View projects" and from appeared list choose MeteoApp section. You should get instantly redirected.

Included locations and measurements

This version include selected meteorology measurements taken directly from multiple sensors installed in the seven biggest cities in Poland (Białystok, Gdańsk, Kraków, Poznań, Szczecin, Warszawa and Wrocław). The measurements parameters are as follows:

# Measurement Unit of measurement Symbol
1 Air temperature Celsius degree ℃
3 Relative humidity expressed as a percentage %
4 Sum of precipitation millimetres mm
5 Pressure hectopascal hPa
6 Wind speed Metre per second m/s
7 Wind direction Degree °
Updates and parameters dependencies

Datetime use in the App is given for Europe / Poland timezone CET/CEST. Data comes from synoptic weather stations located in Poland, administered by Institute of Meteorology and Water Management. New measurements should be present ten minutes approximately after each hour, however once in a while some delays occurs and thus fresh measurement may not be available. To prevent missings on data, new measurement where set to be displayed 15 minutes after each hour.

A datatable contains last 24 captured measurements while main line chart on the right side presents a bit wider time horison. Parameter selectors like 'measurement' and 'city' has dependencies as shown in graph below.

The wind gauge react only by changing 'city' selector value, it gives last measurement of wind speed so this measurement doesn't appear on the line chart. Better outlook of wind speed and direction gives the polar chart that shows measurements distribution of wider time horizon.

Please bear in mind that forecast can be display for any weather parameters or cities but it applies only for air temperature in city of Warsaw.

World map with daily statistics on Covid-19

July 10, 2020

Last modified Aug 25, 2020

When countries ease pandemic-related restrictions that strangled their citizens everyday life for months, people rapidly but unsurely return to their workplace and social life activities. In the times of coronavirus post-outbreak some may forgot about the how dangerous this novel virus is and thus stick with epidemic restrictions as everyone is missing those normal daily life. Are we currently dealing with another wave of the pandemic? You can check it on this map. Just for quick reminder, be responsible, prevent spreading the virus by wearing masks, avoid crowded and enclosed places, keep social distance at least 2 meters away from other person and monitor daily scale of infection in your country.

I've created web map which presents daily confirmed cases of covid19 given for each country. It include indicators like:

  • daily amount of confirmed new cases,
  • historical daily amount of confirmed new cases,
  • number of total reported cases,
  • total cases per million citizens,
  • total cases of deaths.

To see this map tap on the upper green bar "View projects" and from appeared list choose Covid-19 Map. You'll get instantly redirected.

All indicators are given as yesterday statistics as they are originated from reports of each country and must had been confirmed before. Historical values are presented on graph, click on any specific country marker to turn the view on. The most important part of data comes from Eurepean Centre for Disease and Control (ECDC). Algorithm is providing the data directly from Our World in Data, a great organization which mission is to make data and research on the world’s largest problems understandable and accessible (read more about them here). The Covid-19 Map should be up to date each day between 10:00 and 12:00 CET/CEST.

On the updated version, the actual and latest new cases of covid19 for Poland has been added on the left down corner of the map, to give possible best outlook of current situation.
