Anomaly detection & EDA using Apache Spark with Python

August 02, 2020

While investigeting anomalies in your dataset you can tempt to select one of a wide range of different kind of methodology of finding outliers. Some of them are based on overall assumptions others can be more specific, one dimensional or multivariate. It's all depends on your kind of dataset and the aim of data diagnosis. At first, the most important thing is to deeply get into your data while doing exploratory data analysis and find out what problems accures in your dataset and what you are dealing with. After that finding the best solution for detecting outliers can be done.

I give an example of exploratory data analysis and detecting outliers based on dataset containing Brasilian houses to rent. The map below points on cities in with the houses to rent are located.

The code has been created using Apache Spark and Python language. You will find there also example of IsolationForest method which in my opinion is one of the most valuable at the moment. It's a tree-based model where partitions are created by first randomly selecting a feature and then selecting random value in range between minimum and maximum of that feature.

That's all for the moment. Tap on the upper green bar "View projects" and from appeared list choose Anomaly detection & EDA example section. You should get instantly redirected to the notepad with code.

Stay tuned, soon I'll update this post with more insights on the Brasilian houses dataset.

I'm glad to announce that the first release of MeteoApp is now available online. It's a non-commercial project which the purpose of analysis publicly shared weather data. Updates and parameters dependencies

The data comes from synoptic weather stations located in Poland, shared by Institute of Meteorology and Water Management. New measurements should be present ten minutes after every hour approximately, however once in a while some delay occurs and new measurement could be unavailable. To prevent missing of data, new measurement should be displayed 15 minutes after the hour.

The table in App contains last 24 captured measurements while line chart presents wider time horison. Parameter selectors like 'measurement' and 'city' has coded dependencies as shown in graph below

The wind gauge react only by changing 'city' selector value, it gives last measurement of wind speed and doesn't appear on the line chart. More overall view gives the polar chart that shows distribution of wind speed and direction of wider time horizon.

World map with daily statistics on Covid-19

July 10, 2020

When countries ease pandemic-related restrictions that strangled their citizens everyday life for months, people rapidly and unsurely return to their workplace and social life activities. In the times of coronavirus post-outbreak some may forgot about the danger lurking around the corner as everyone is missing those normal daily life. In my country media has stopped to provides corona-brief independently if indicator of new cases is rising or dropping. How hogh is today? Do we deal with another wava of the pandemic? Well.. I encourage you to track it by yourself. Just for quick reminder, be responsible, prevent spreading the virus by wearing masks, avoid crowded and enclosed places, keep social distance at least 2 meters away from other person especially from those who are coughing, or sneezing and daily monitor the impact of infection rate in your country.

I've created a simple web map which present daily confirmed cases reports given for each country. You'll find there indicators like:

  • daily amount of confirmed new cases,
  • historical daily amount of confirmed new cases,
  • number of total reported cases,
  • total cases per million citizens,
  • total cases of deaths.

Just tap on the upper green bar "View projects" and from appeared list choose Covid-19 Map section. You should get instantly redirected to the map.

All indicators are given as yesterday statistics as they are originated from each country and must have been confirmed. To view historical values of new cases click on the specific counrty marker. The most important part of data comes from Eurepean Centre for Disease and Control (ECDC). Algorithm is providing the data directly from Our World in Data, great organization which mission is to make data and research on the worldâ€™s largest problems understandable and accessible (read more about them here). The Covid-19 Map should be updated daily between 10:00 and 12:00 CEST.

I hope that you find this map helpful for tracking the indicators of current cases of Covid-19 and will contribute for overall safe and health.
